# Portfolio Foundation

This document defines the direction before rebuilding the site in larger chunks. The reference point is the story-led structure of arjun-r.com: the site quickly explains who the designer is, proves it through work, then reveals how they think. This portfolio should follow that level of clarity and personality without copying the visual style.

## First 10 Seconds

People should immediately understand:

> I design clean, interactive experiences — and I obsess over the small moments that make them feel right.

The first screen should make recruiters or product teams feel:

- This person is an interaction designer, not a general visual designer.
- They think like a product designer: systems, flows, behavior, tradeoffs.
- They have motion craft, but use it to improve feel and clarity.
- The taste level is minimal, precise, and controlled.
- There is a quiet intelligence to the site: small delightful moments, not loud effects.

## Positioning

Sudhanshu Singh is an interaction designer with product thinking.

Motion is a craft in the toolkit. It should support usability, state, feedback, transition, and emotional pacing. It should not make the portfolio feel like a motion designer reel.

Short version:

> Interaction designer with product thinking, shaping clean digital experiences through systems, behavior, and small moments of motion.

## Audience

Primary audience:

- Product-first companies hiring full-time product or interaction designers.
- Design teams looking for someone who can think through product behavior, not just make screens.
- AI/product teams that value clarity in complex workflows.

Secondary audience:

- Creative/product collaborators who need someone with good taste and execution.
- Event or brand teams only when the work supports interaction/product credibility.

## Personality

The site should feel:

- Clean
- Clever
- Precise
- Product-minded
- Minimal but not empty
- Calm but not warm
- Confident but not loud

Avoid:

- Overly friendly copy
- Decorative gradients
- Portfolio-template project cards
- Huge generic slogans
- Motion for spectacle
- Too much black-on-white harshness

Use:

- Neutral value shifts
- Tiny labels
- Crisp grid
- Times New Roman italic for rare emphasis
- Smooth hover and scroll behavior
- Glass only where it helps hierarchy
- Micro-interactions that feel useful or witty

## Homepage Story Arc

The homepage should work like a short designed argument:

1. **Who I am**
   A precise hero line that defines interaction design + product thinking.

2. **What I care about**
   Small moments, behavior, clarity, interface feel.

3. **Proof through projects**
   Waysorted, Mazout, Solace as the main three. These should feel like chapters, not just links.

4. **How I think**
   A dedicated section about product behavior, systems, motion as feedback, and designing for clarity.

5. **Experience signal**
   Resume-level credibility: Waysorted, Mazout, Gen AI Summit, education, tools.

6. **Contact**
   Simple and direct. No over-explaining.

## Project Page Structure

Each project page should eventually follow this structure:

1. Project title + one-line value
2. Role / timeline / tools / team
3. Problem
4. Product behavior or design challenge
5. Process highlights
6. Key decisions
7. Final outcome
8. What I learned
9. Next project

Waysorted should be the first fully built case study.

Mazout and Solace can start as lighter project pages, then grow once assets and notes are available.

## Interaction Principles

Interactions should feel like product behavior:

- Hover states reveal meaning.
- Page transitions should feel calm.
- Scroll reveals should move, not hide content.
- Cursor labels are allowed, but should be subtle.
- Glass should be used for hierarchy and state, not decoration.
- Motion should be quick, restrained, and purposeful.

## Visual Principles

Use:

- Urbanist for the main type system.
- Times New Roman italic for rare emphasis.
- Off-white, warm grey, charcoal, soft glass.
- Mostly single-column or two-column editorial grids.
- Thin rules and enough breathing room.

Avoid:

- Blue/purple gradients
- Heavy dark sections everywhere
- Big decorative cards
- Stock-like visuals
- Loud animation
- Too many rounded pills
