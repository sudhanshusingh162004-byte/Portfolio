Case study 01

Waysorted

Waysorted is an AI workspace where users coordinate agents, files, tasks, and outputs. My design focus was to make the system feel structured: clear enough for fast execution, but detailed enough for review and trust.

Role
Product strategy, UX, motion design
Tools
Figma, Rive, After Effects, Antigravity
Timeline
2025 - Present
Problem

AI products can become visually busy very quickly. Users need to understand what is active, what the system knows, and what decision comes next without reading every control on the screen.

Principle

I treated clarity as a hierarchy problem. Primary intent stays visible, supporting context is layered in, and advanced details appear only when the user is ready to inspect them.

Motion

Motion was used as product language: transitions explain state, pacing reduces abruptness, and small moments of feedback help users read what the agent is doing.

Outcome

The result is a calmer workspace model: reduced visual noise, progressive disclosure for complex flows, and navigation patterns that make agent-driven tasks easier to follow.

79% Task completion lift in tested flows
64% Reduction in reported cognitive load
93% Judged state-aware by reviewers
